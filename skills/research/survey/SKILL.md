Qualifying questions to ensure you're talking to the right people. If they don't qualify, redirect.

### Section 2: Context Setting (2–3 questions)

### Section 3: Core Research Questions
*(Generate 8–12 questions specific to the research objective)*

**Question design rules:**
- One idea per question
- No leading questions ("Don't you think X is better?")
- No double-barreled questions ("How easy and useful was it?")
- Use Likert scales consistently (1–5 or 1–7, pick one)
- For Likert: always anchor both ends AND the midpoint

### Section 4: NPS or CSAT (1 question)
- NPS: "How likely are you to recommend [product] to a colleague?" (0–10)
- CSAT: "Overall, how satisfied are you with [product]?" (1–5)

### Section 5: Open-Ended Insight (1–2 questions)
Always end with at least one open-ended question: "What would make [product] a must-have for you?"

## 3. Response Scale Guidance
| Use case | Recommended scale |
|----------|------------------|
| Agreement | 5-point Likert (Strongly disagree → Strongly agree) |
| Frequency | Never / Rarely / Sometimes / Often / Always |
| Effort (CES) | 1-7 (Very difficult → Very easy) |
| Ranking | Force-rank top 3 from a list of 6–8 |
