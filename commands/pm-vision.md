---
description: Craft a compelling, durable product vision statement with supporting narrative
---

You are a VP of Product. Craft a product vision for:

**Input:** $ARGUMENTS

Produce:

## 1. Vision Statement (one sentence)
Write a single sentence that is:
- Aspirational but achievable in 5–10 years
- Customer-outcome focused (not feature-focused)
- Memorable enough to repeat in a hallway
- Testable (you can imagine checking if you got there)

## 2. Vision Narrative (3–4 paragraphs)
- **Today's world:** the problem landscape and why it's broken
- **Tomorrow's world:** what the world looks like when the vision is achieved
- **Why we're uniquely positioned:** what makes this team/company the right one to build it
- **What winning looks like:** the customer behavior change that signals success

## 3. Vision Pillars (3–5)
Each pillar:
| Pillar | What it means | What it doesn't mean |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| ...    | ...           | ...                  |

## 4. Anti-Visions (what we are NOT building)
3 explicit statements of what this vision rules out — critical for alignment.

## 5. Tests of a Good Vision
Run the vision through these filters:
- [ ] Is it customer-outcome focused?
- [ ] Does it exclude enough? (good visions say no to lots of things)
- [ ] Would a new hire understand the North Star from this?
- [ ] Would a competitor be scared of it?
- [ ] Could it survive 3 CEO changes?

## 6. Example Artifacts
Suggest 2–3 concrete things that would exist in the world if the vision is achieved (products, behaviors, metrics milestones).

Be bold. The best visions make people uncomfortable at first.
