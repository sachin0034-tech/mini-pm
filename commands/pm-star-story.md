---

### T — Task (10% of story, ~15 seconds)
Your specific role and responsibility:
- What were YOU responsible for? (Not the team — you)
- What was the constraint? (time, resources, ambiguity)
- What would "success" have meant?

*Draft:*
[1–2 sentences. Use "I" not "we."]

---

### A — Action (50% of story, ~60 seconds)
This is where you win or lose the interview.
- **Step 1:** What did you do first and why?
- **Step 2:** What was the insight or decision that changed things?
- **Step 3:** How did you execute / influence others?
- **What you specifically did** (not the team)

*Draft:*
[4–6 sentences. Show judgment and leadership. The interviewer wants to see HOW you think, not just WHAT happened.]

---

### R — Result (20% of story, ~30 seconds)
Quantify. Contextualize. Reflect.
- **Quantified outcome:** (revenue, users, time saved, accuracy)
- **Scope:** (how many users, what scale, what timeframe)
- **Recognition:** (exec shoutout, award, repeat investment)
- **What you'd do differently:**

*Draft:*
[2–3 sentences. If you don't have a number, describe the qualitative impact clearly and note why measurement was hard.]
